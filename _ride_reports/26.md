A, J and I woke up in Girona and picked up rental bikes at a nice spot called [Tomata](https://tomatabikerental.com/) nearby. We didn't know exactly what to expect in terms of the bikes or the bags that we'd have to work with. In the end we had to downsize a bit, leaving the rest of our things with the rental folks. I was glad to have brought along a bit of my own odds and ends (handlebar bag, mini frame bag etc) from home. 

After we got packed up, we set off for A's farm near Santa Pau. It was a longer but fun ride, mostly on [the Carrilet greenway](https://www.viesverdes.cat/en/rutes_vies_verdes/narrow-gauge-railway-route-i/) which runs between Girona and Olot. We got off around Les Preses and headed for Santa Pau area, still mainly on trails.

The ride was mostly gradual uphill, with a few bigger climbs and descents mixed in towards the end especially. There were plenty of spots to stop along the way for both fun and functional purposes. Here was a pump track we came across and rode with our fully loaded bikes:

![Pump track spotted along the Carrilet greenway](/figs/ride_reports/26/pump_track.jpg){:style="display:block;margin:0 auto;width:70%;"}

Overall the bikes worked out pretty well; they were some carbon gravel bikes set up 1x, similarly geared to my Bombora. I didn't love the thick carbon frame aesthetic, but they were definitely nice and functional and on the lighter side.
