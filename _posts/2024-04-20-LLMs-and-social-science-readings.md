An evolving reading list capturing work on the use of LLMs in social science. Feel free to contribute via PR or by messaging me at `fossj117 AT gmail DOT com`. 

## Perspectives / Methods

* [Large Language Models as Simulated Economic Agents: What Can We Learn from Homo Silicus?](https://arxiv.org/abs/2301.07543)
* [Automated Social Science: Language Models as Scientist and Subjects](https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.11794)
* [Using large language models in psychology](https://www.nature.com/articles/s44159-023-00241-5.epdf)

## Reviews

* [Large Language Models as Subpopulation Representative Models: A Review](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.17888)

## Applications

### Human Behavior

* [Simulating Human Strategic Behavior: Comparing Single and Multi-agent LLMs](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2402.08189.pdf)

* [Do AIs know what the most important issue is? Using language models to code open-text social survey responses at scale](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/full/10.1177/20531680241231468)
